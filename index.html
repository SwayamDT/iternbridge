The Challenge We All Face

Our Managers Are Stretched.
Our Interns Deserve Better.

We all want our interns to succeed. But with growing workloads and limited bandwidth, even the best-intentioned onboarding process leaves gaps. Here is what we are seeing across the board.

"We have talented interns joining us every batch, but our managers are already at capacity. The result? Interns spend their first weeks figuring things out on their own — and managers wish they could do more."

Unclear Starting Point

Interns arrive eager but unsure where to begin. Without a shared roadmap, everyone interprets expectations differently.

Managers Are Overloaded

Our teams are already managing deadlines and client deliverables. Repeated orientation conversations simply do not fit into packed schedules.

Knowledge Stays with Individuals

Valuable know-how — processes, frameworks, terminology — lives in people's heads. When they are busy, that knowledge is inaccessible.

No Shared Visibility

Neither interns nor managers have a clear, objective view of where the onboarding journey stands. Progress goes unnoticed.

Learning Goes Undocumented

Interns leave with skills and experience, but nothing tangible to show for it — no portfolio, no record of growth.

Valuable Time Slips Away

Weeks of potential contribution are lost to confusion and waiting. The investment in our intern program does not fully pay off.
